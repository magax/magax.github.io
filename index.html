About me

Hello, I am Michael Glass, a cat-loving human (Most likely…), majoring in Computer Engineering at Kettering University in, Flint, Michigan. I was born and raised in Knoxville, Tennessee, a long way away, and a few degrees warmer (on average) then my new residence. I always knew I wanted to be an Engineer (Everyone says it, but it is true!). As I went into High school though, I did not know really type I wanted to be. I enjoyed programming and building things out of wood. In my high school, which was a stand alone STEM magnet school, one of my teachers introduced me to Microcontrollers. By introduced I mean he gave me a Microchip DemoBoard with a PIC16f616, its data sheet, and some basic lessons in assembly. I was hooked. The interacting between the code and the physical world was amazing to me. The next year I took his class that used embedded C, a different version of the Microchip Demo board that had an LCD display, and used the PIC16f1937. That love has propelled me to Kettering University to seek a degree in Computer Engineering. My goal is to work as an embedded systems engineer.

Patient Alert System

A System built to alert Doctor's to a patient's arrival.

My father's had noticed that in a few local doctor's offices, they had a system that used buttons and lights to alert the doctors when their patients had arrived. He asked me if I would be able to create a similar device. I had a basic understanding of Adruino coding, so I started to research what I would need. I already had most of the components for a basic prototype, so I wired up an Adrunio and tested it. With approval from my father and his coworkers, I purchased the required components and assembled the unit. A few months after the initial instillation, a few of the LED’s used for the project had blown. I went back and replaced the original resistors with different ones, with higher resistance.
This project had one “main” unit in the office’s lobby, with 3 buttons and 3 LED’s with resistors, and one “office” unit in each of the three offices with 1 LED with resistor, and one button each. The buttons in the main unit were only capable of turning on the LED’s that corresponded with it. The button in the office was only capable of turning back off the corresponding LED’s.
